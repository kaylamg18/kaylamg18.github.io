Drafty: Design & Manufacturing



The project

Drafty was an engineering project for ME203: Design and Manufacturing, a class where students are challenged
to create anything as long as it used several manufacturing processes. My project was a lightbox tracing table
with folding legs. Below you can see some of my initial sketches.







The challenge

ME203: Design and Manufacturing pushed my limits and comfort level in the PRL and as an engineer. I had never
had so much freedom and flexibility in a project, so by the time I had hand crafted an entire table, it meant I had
made hundreds of small decisions. Every part I decided to buy, every hole I decided to drill, even every grain of
sandpaper I decided to use was deeply thought out during the process.































The Prototype

I first created a functional prototype to demonstrate how my idea could look and to see if it was practical. The
prototype helped me see how I could simplify my design and what challenges I might face as I began my
manufacturing processes.





Process

Below are some of my process photos from my time in the Stanford Product Realization Lab. I worked
hard over 10 weeks to create something I am deeply proud of. Every detail of this project was an incredible learning
opportunity and I finished the quarter with a new appreciation for everyday items.
