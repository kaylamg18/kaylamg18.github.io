SMEG Flashlight Design



The project

This project is a reimagined flashlight, designed to fit
the brand image of an Italian home appliance manufacturer with a
great retro feel, called Smeg. I decided that the target user would be young,
middle class, "hipsters" who liked and could afford unique products. I
studied the brand and prototyped a final design.





















The challenge

Designing a flashlight with a specific user group and brand in
mind challenged me to truly understand what was essential to the
design. Flashlights come in all shapes and sizes, but this one
had to be designed to evoke specific feelings and ideas. Creating
an innovative design that felt like it fit into the brand family
was a creative challenge that taught me how to make something new
using established patterns.





















The process

Once I understood who I was designing for and had put together
inspiration boards, I began ideation through drawing. I sketched
out numerous drawings, letting my imagination run wild before
choosing what represented the brand best. Some sketches had
shapes I liked while others had superior features. Eventually my
ideas culminated into one strong design.













































































Prototyping

I mapped out my final design and hit the Product Realization Lab
in order to machine my physical prototype. Building the prototype was
a great way to gain experience with a new material and machining parts.
My project required two pieces put together, which I did using a small
interior rod.
















































Final Product

Finally, all that was left was to make the final prototype look a
little more professional using photoshop. The last two photos show
my final build and a photoshopped mockup of the flashlight if it were
to become an actual product.
